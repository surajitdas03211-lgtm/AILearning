# Brake Failure — Emergency Driver Procedure

## If Brake Pedal Goes to the Floor (no resistance)
1. Pump the pedal rapidly 4-6 times. Hydraulic systems sometimes restore pressure momentarily.
2. Downshift aggressively to use engine braking — manual: drop two gears; auto: use sport or manual mode.
3. Apply parking brake gradually. Sudden full application can lock rear wheels and skid.
4. Steer toward an uphill grade, soft shoulder, sand/gravel runaway lane, or open space.
5. Sound horn continuously and flash headlights to warn other traffic.
6. Aim for cushioning material (bushes, fence, soft barriers) rather than solid objects if collision is unavoidable.

## If Brakes Pull to One Side
- One caliper is dragging or one hydraulic circuit has lost pressure.
- Reduce speed using engine braking, then bring to a controlled stop.
- Do not continue driving. The vehicle is not safely roadworthy.

## If Pedal is Hard, Brakes Weak
- Vacuum assist failure (brake booster, vacuum hose, or check valve).
- Increase pedal pressure with both feet if needed.
- Engine braking remains effective; use it.

## ABS Warning Light On
- ABS is offline, but base braking remains functional.
- Drive cautiously to the nearest workshop. Avoid hard braking, especially in wet conditions.
- This is not an emergency, but the vehicle should not continue long-haul service until repaired.

## After Any Brake Failure
The vehicle must be towed, not driven, to the workshop. A "self-recovered" brake system is unreliable and may fail again under load. The entire brake system — pads, lines, master cylinder, booster — should be inspected before returning to service.
